# 2.3 Public Website Information Architecture (v0.1)

## Purpose
- Define public-facing IA for discoverability, trust, conversion, and clear handoff to operational workflows.

## Scope
- Covers non-authenticated pages and their relationship to lead intake and intelligence publishing.
- Excludes command center internal IA.

## Key decisions
- IA is organized by audience intent: understand, evaluate, trust, engage.
- Service detail pages remain separate (no page merging) to preserve campaign and SEO specificity.
- Intelligence/news content has dual tracks: public newsroom feed and restricted intelligence onboarding.
- Legal/compliance pages remain globally reachable and consistently linked in footer/navigation.

## IA hierarchy (public)
- `Home`
- Value proposition, key metrics, cross-domain pathway tiles, emergency contact CTA.

- `About`
- Mission/vision/timeline and enterprise trust positioning.

- `Services`
- Service hub:
- Threat Monitoring
- Incident Response
- Compliance Management
- Federal Compliance
- Asset Recovery

- `Proof & Trust`
- Case Studies
- Teams
- Portfolio (preview)
- Sentinel Trust (framework narrative)
- QFS (domain narrative)
- Alliance Trust bridge page

- `Intelligence`
- News (public newsroom feed + categories)
- Intelligence (restricted-access positioning and onboarding request)
- Resources (guides/reports/tools + subscription entry)

- `Conversion`
- Pricing
- Contact Us

- `Legal`
- Privacy Policy
- Terms of Service
- Cookie Policy

## Navigation model (public)
- Primary nav: Home, About, Services, Intelligence, Team, Contact.
- Secondary discovery: Case Studies, Resources, Pricing, Portfolio, domain-specific pages.
- Footer: legal, service quick links, resource links, emergency contact.
- Persistent support affordance: live support widget for departmental routing.

## Conversion paths (public -> operational)
- Service detail CTA -> Contact form -> Intake record in command center inbox.
- News/resources subscription -> Subscriber record -> newsroom campaign flow.
- Restricted intelligence request -> gated review queue (future operational queue).
- Emergency hotline CTA -> out-of-band IR path (phone-driven, potential future ticket intake integration).

## Content governance model
- Public copy treated as controlled content with periodic compliance/legal review.
- Intelligence feed requires editorial lifecycle and source integrity checks.
- Claims with regulatory implications (SLA, compliance badges, audit language) require approval workflow.

## Assumptions (explicit)
- Public IA must support both cybersecurity and physical asset protection narratives.
- Public and restricted intelligence experiences remain separate at v0.1.
- Existing route structure is retained; IA refinement is additive and non-destructive.

## Non-blocking open questions
- Whether `Portfolio` should remain public preview or move behind authenticated access.
- Whether `QFS` and `Sentinel` are standalone product families or service sub-pillars.
- Whether region-specific legal pages are needed before launch expansion.
