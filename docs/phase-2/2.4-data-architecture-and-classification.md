# 2.4 Data Architecture & Classification (v0.1)

## Purpose
- Define data domains, classification levels, lifecycle controls, and security/compliance handling expectations.

## Scope
- Covers operational, identity, content, and audit datasets across public and authenticated surfaces.
- Excludes vendor-specific infrastructure and encryption implementation specifics.

## Key decisions
- Data is tenant-scoped by default for operational domains.
- Audit and compliance evidence records are immutable/append-only.
- Security operations data is retained with stricter access controls than marketing data.
- Classification-driven controls determine encryption, retention, and export constraints.

## Data domains
- `Identity & Access Data`
- Users, roles, permissions, grants, sessions, tenant/org/team relationships.

- `Operational Security Data`
- Alerts, incidents, SLA profiles/breaches, on-call schedules, escalation outcomes.

- `Intake & Engagement Data`
- Contact messages, newsletter subscribers, campaign artifacts.

- `Content & Intelligence Data`
- News articles, metadata/tags, editorial states, publication logs.

- `Audit & Compliance Data`
- Audit events, control evidence, policy changes, export logs.

## Classification taxonomy
- `Public`
- Example: marketing page copy, public article summaries.
- Controls: integrity monitoring, standard backup.

- `Internal`
- Example: diagnostics summaries, non-sensitive operational metadata.
- Controls: authenticated access, least privilege, transport encryption.

- `Confidential`
- Example: contact messages, subscriber lists, incident records, role grants.
- Controls: strict RBAC, field-level access minimization, encrypted at rest, audited reads/writes.

- `Restricted`
- Example: high-severity incident details, evidence artifacts, audit exports, policy decisions.
- Controls: break-glass governance, immutable logging, export controls, enhanced retention/legal hold workflows.

## Data lifecycle requirements
- Ingestion
- Validate schema, enforce tenant context, attach actor/request metadata.

- Storage
- Segregate by domain and classification; avoid co-mingling restricted and public datasets.

- Processing
- Event-driven transitions for incidents/SLA/escalation/news campaigns.

- Access
- Policy-based authorization with tenant scope and role constraints.

- Retention
- Audit/compliance and incident records retain longer than marketing analytics.
- Support legal hold and tamper-evident retention for restricted data.

- Disposal
- Controlled purge with deletion proofs where regulatory scope permits.

## Control mapping (high-level)
- Confidential/Restricted data:
- Encryption at rest and in transit.
- Immutable audit traces for sensitive mutations.
- Export approvals and traceable download actions.
- Tenant isolation checks on all authenticated APIs.

- Public/Internal data:
- Integrity controls and change tracking.
- Lower retention burden unless linked to compliance evidence.

## Assumptions (explicit)
- Multi-tenant isolation is mandatory.
- GEM-operated security controls are authoritative at launch.
- No day-one customer-managed key mandate is assumed.
- No immediate EU-only residency hard requirement is assumed in v0.1.

## Non-blocking open questions
- Final retention schedules by data class and regulation set.
- Whether contact/subscriber data requires region-specific residency before scale-up.
- Required granularity of immutable hashing/signing for audit exports.
