# 2.1 Domain Model & Bounded Contexts (v0.1)

## Purpose
- Define the core enterprise domains for GEM Cyber.
- Establish clear bounded contexts, ownership boundaries, and cross-domain dependencies.

## Scope
- Includes cybersecurity operations, IAM, compliance/governance, newsroom publishing, and platform shared services.
- Excludes implementation-level schemas and deployment topology details.

## Key decisions
- Bounded contexts are separated by business capability, not UI pages.
- Incident lifecycle is centered in `Incident Response`, with `Threat Monitoring` as upstream signal source.
- `IAM` is a platform-wide policy decision point for all privileged and tenant-scoped operations.
- `Audit & Event Backbone` is cross-cutting and append-only by default for sensitive actions.
- `Newsletter/Intelligence Publishing` is decoupled from public rendering to support editorial workflow and campaign scheduling.

## Domain model (high-level)
- `Tenant`: root organizational boundary for isolation and policy scoping.
- `Organization`: tenant-owned business unit/customer account.
- `User`: actor identity with role assignments and session context.
- `Role`, `Permission`, `Grant`: authorization model.
- `Alert`: normalized detection event from monitoring/intel sources.
- `Incident`: managed security event with severity, SLA profile, and response status.
- `SlaProfile`, `SlaBreach`: contractual response and breach tracking.
- `OnCallSchedule`, `EscalationPolicy`: routing and escalation controls.
- `ContactMessage`: intake artifact from public channels.
- `Subscriber`, `Campaign`: intelligence/newsletter publishing entities.
- `ComplianceFramework`, `Control`, `Evidence`: governance and attestation.
- `AuditEvent`: immutable record of sensitive operations and decisions.

## Bounded contexts
- `Threat Monitoring`
- Responsibilities: alert ingestion, enrichment, severity classification, correlation.
- Inputs: telemetry, intel feeds, watchlists.
- Outputs: normalized alerts, escalation triggers.
- Depends on: IAM (access scope), Audit/Event Backbone.

- `Incident Response`
- Responsibilities: case creation, lifecycle tracking, containment/resolution workflow.
- Inputs: escalated alerts, analyst declarations.
- Outputs: incidents, response tasks, closure records.
- Depends on: Threat Monitoring, SLA, On-Call, Audit/Event Backbone.

- `SLA & Breach Detection`
- Responsibilities: response clocks, warning thresholds, breach adjudication.
- Inputs: incident timestamps/state transitions.
- Outputs: warning and breach events, compliance-facing logs.
- Depends on: Incident Response, Audit/Event Backbone.

- `On-Call & Escalation`
- Responsibilities: rotation ownership, paging flow, acknowledgement state.
- Inputs: alerts/incidents/SLA warnings.
- Outputs: page events, escalation outcomes.
- Depends on: IAM, Incident Response, SLA, Audit/Event Backbone.

- `IAM (Roles, Permissions, Tenants)`
- Responsibilities: identity lifecycle, access policy, tenant scoping.
- Inputs: user/team/org changes, auth assertions.
- Outputs: policy decisions, role grants, access tokens/sessions.
- Depends on: Audit/Event Backbone.

- `Compliance & Governance`
- Responsibilities: controls mapping, evidence lifecycle, readiness posture, audit exports.
- Inputs: incidents, system events, policy updates.
- Outputs: control assessments, evidence bundles, findings.
- Depends on: Incident Response, Audit/Event Backbone, IAM.

- `Newsletter / Intelligence Publishing`
- Responsibilities: editorial pipeline, campaign scheduling, subscriber segmentation.
- Inputs: curated content, approvals, segmentation rules.
- Outputs: published stories, campaign dispatch records.
- Depends on: IAM, Audit/Event Backbone.

- `Audit & Event Backbone`
- Responsibilities: append-only event log, query/export, retention governance.
- Inputs: domain events across contexts.
- Outputs: immutable audit ledger, forensic replay surfaces.
- Depends on: secure storage, key management services.

## Assumptions (explicit)
- Multi-tenant model is required and remains in scope.
- GEM operates the platform (not customer-managed infrastructure by default).
- Compliance baseline includes SOC2/ISO/NIST-aligned controls with tenant-scoped evidence handling.
- Event-driven integration is required between operational domains.

## Non-blocking open questions
- Whether `Asset Recovery` remains a first-class bounded context or stays under service-layer orchestration.
- Whether newsroom segmentation requires per-tenant branding at launch.
- Required RTO/RPO targets for audit and incident data.
